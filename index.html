OLIVIA

I'm Olivia Wilson, a fashion designer who works with structure, fabric, and small details that most people overlook. I like clothes that feel considered, not rushed. My work leans toward clean lines, but I don't avoid risk when a piece needs it.

I design with people in mind, not trends. Every project starts with a person's habits, their movement, and what they actually wear day to day. That's what shapes the final piece more than anything else.

MY
STORY

I didn't start in a studio. I started by altering my own clothes because nothing fit the way I wanted. That turned into making pieces from scratch, then taking on small client work. It grew slowly, but it stayed honest.

There wasn't a single turning point. It was a series of small jobs, late nights, and mistakes I had to fix myself. Over time, I built a process that works for me and for the people I design for.

WHAT I
CAN DO
FOR YOU
1

I design clothing that fits your body and your routine. Whether it's a single piece or a small collection, I focus on how it sits, moves, and lasts over time.

2

I can take an idea and shape it into something wearable. If you already have a concept, I refine it. If you don't, I help you build one from the ground up.

THE WAY I WORK

I start with a conversation. I ask direct questions about how you live and what you need from your clothes. That saves time later.

After that, I sketch and test. I don't rush to final pieces. I'd rather adjust early than fix something after it's already made.

I keep the process clear. You'll know what stage we're in, what changes are being made, and why they matter.

EDUCATIONAL
BACKGROUND

I studied fashion design with a focus on construction and pattern-making. Most of my time was spent learning how garments actually come together, not just how they look.

Outside formal study, I worked with local tailors and small workshops. That hands-on work shaped how I approach fit and finishing.

I continue to learn through practice. Each project adds something new, whether it's a technique, a fabric, or a better way to solve a problem.

OTHER
SERVICES

I offer clothing alterations for pieces that need adjustment but not a full redesign. This includes resizing, reshaping, and small structural fixes.

I provide styling guidance for clients who want a more consistent wardrobe. The focus is on pieces that work together without overcomplicating things.

I help develop small fashion lines for new brands. This covers design, sampling, and preparing pieces for production.

I also consult on fabric selection and sourcing. Choosing the right material early prevents problems later in the process.
